As an open major

What is it?

An Interactive Workshop Designed To Equip First-Year Open Majors For Success. Every year about 20% of new students enter the university as a declared “open major.” It can be overwhelming to sort through the wealth of information and opinions new students receive while trying to select a specific major. This workshop will encourage first-year students to explore their interests, values, and abilities as well as discover campus resources to help in identifying potential academic and career paths.

Career Assessment: Strong Interest Inventory
Taking a career assessment is a first step in the career exploration process.  Assessments are designed to help you understand how your interests align with various majors and occupations. While no assessment can perfectly capture who you are or predict what occupation you’ll pursue, they can help you narrow choices and discover options you may not have considered.  The Strong Interest Inventory is an informative and detailed assessment that compares your likes and dislikes to professionals in specific occupations.

Benefits of Campus Engagement

Wrapping It Up and Moving Forward
As we wrap up our two days together, this will be an opportunity to ask and discuss any lingering questions about entering the University of Iowa community as an open major. We’ll seize this opportunity to affirm that it’s okay to be an open major and to explore ways to manage pressure from parents and others about selecting a major. Participants will identify two to three personalized commitments to themselves that will make the transition to the University of Iowa the very best possible!
